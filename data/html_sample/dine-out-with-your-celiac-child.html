How to Dine out With Your Celiac Child

  • 1,395 views

  • 6 Editors

  • Edited

Parents who have children who suffer from Celiac disease may be hard pressed to find restaurants that offer healthy options for their children to safely consume. If a child with a gluten allergy consumes wheat, even in small doses, either intentionally or accidentally, it can cause serious health problems.

  • Gluten affects the intestines of Celiac patients by preventing them from absorbing crucial nutrients and causing nausea, fatigue and severe diarrhea.
  • If you want to eat out with your Celiac child, there are some important steps you can follow to facilitate a safe dining experience away from the home.
Ad

Steps

  1. 1
    Bring a detailed list of acceptable and prohibited foods with you to the restaurant. Create a laminated list of foods that contain gluten for easy reference.
    • Keeping this list on you at all times will ensure that you are ready to provide anyone with information about Celiac disease or gluten sensitivity
    Ad
  2. 2
    Go online and check the menu. Some restaurants actually have gluten free options. But, this, of course, depends on the type of restaurant and food.
  3. 3
    Ask for a hamburger with no bun! You can do this at almost any place, just like asking for no mayo!
  4. 4
    Ask about gluten-free options and ingredients.

Tips

  • Call a restaurant twice before you visit with your gluten sensitive child. Make the first call several days ahead to see if the restaurant is a viable choice for your family, and call once again on the day you will be dining at the restaurant.
  • Notify the restaurant in advance about which day you will be eating out so that the management can be sure that gluten-free menu ingredients are available.
Ad

Warnings

  • Do not let your child share food off of your plate as some of your food may be contaminated with gluten-containing food items.
  • Avoid cream sauces, cheese sauces, gravy, and all fried foods as these often contain flour which is used as a thickening agent or a crisp outer coating.
